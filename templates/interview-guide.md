# Customer Interview Guide

## Before the Interview

### Prep
- [ ] Research the person (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- [ ] Review their usage data (if available)
- [ ] Prepare recording setup
- [ ] Have product ready for demo if needed

### Mindset
- You're here to **listen**, not sell
- Their problems > your solution
- Silence is okay — let them think
- No leading questions

---

## Interview Structure (30 min)

### 1. Warm-up (2 min)
- "Thanks for taking the time!"
- "I'm trying to understand [problem space] better"
- "No wrong answers — I want your honest experience"

### 2. Context (5 min)
- "Tell me about your role / day-to-day"
- "When did you last deal with [problem]?"
- "Walk me through what happened"

### 3. Problem Deep-dive (10 min)
- "What's the hardest part about [problem]?"
- "Why is that hard?"
- "What have you tried before?"
- "Why didn't that work?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"

### 4. Current Solutions (5 min)
- "What do you use now?"
- "What do you like about it?"
- "What do you wish was different?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand..."

### 5. Product Feedback (5 min)
*Only if they've used your product*
- "What made you try [product]?"
- "What's working well?"
- "What's frustrating?"
- "Would you recommend it? Why/why not?"

### 6. Wrap-up (3 min)
- "Anything else I should know?"
- "Can I follow up if I have questions?"
- "Know anyone else with this problem?"

## After the Interview

### Immediately
- [ ] Write raw notes while fresh
- [ ] Capture exact quotes
- [ ] Note emotional moments

### Claude Code Analysis Prompt

```
Analyze this customer interview transcript:

[paste transcript]

Extract:
1. Main pain points (with quotes)
2. Current solutions they use
3. What they value most
4. Objections or concerns
5. Feature requests (explicit and implicit)
6. Willingness to pay signals
7. Referral potential

Format as actionable insights for product development.
```

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ "Would you use a product that..."
❌ "Don't you think it would be great if..."
❌ "Most people say..."
❌ Talking more than 30% of the time
❌ Defending your product
❌ Asking about future behavior ("Would you pay...")

## Green Flags

✅ "Tell me more about that"
✅ "Why?"
✅ "What happened next?"
✅ "How did that make you feel?"
✅ Long silences where they think
✅ Stories, not opinions
